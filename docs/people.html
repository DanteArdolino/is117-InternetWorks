Important People

Chad Hurley - Co-founder of insanely popular website Youtube the online video sharing service that was bought by Google for 1.65 billion dollars. “The internet is about connecting the individuals to information” Promoted the aura of the TV industry being mobile and in the hands of users

Jay Adelson - Co-Founder of Digg a social news website of which the content is chosen by its user community of millions. People were allowed to upvote or downvote the content that was displayed and this application gained heavy traffic. Diggs popularity went on to promote other sites such as the now very successful and popular Reddit.

Mark Zucherberg - The 22 year old founder's opinions mean a lot when it comes from peers and family and built Facebook on the basis of these ideals. Originally did not intend on building a business but rather a virtual place in which people can share their stories and be in constant interconnectedness.

Frank Sasser- Music lover who with his peers put together tunes with PCs and the web. Thought people were very unfamiliar with the idea of people playing music from computers and so further pursued his passion. Created Audion and Winamp apps that allowed users to store and play music on their computers in the form of MP3s

Shawn Fanning - Created Napster, a platform in which people could share their own music collections in the structure of a peer to peer format. Napster allowed its users to let their computers talk to each other and play mp3s by searching for the name of a song, downloading the software straight from the others PC. Napster was shut down after backlash from the music industries, paving the way for Apple Music's market.
