Domain names and ICANN

ICANN - Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers A domain name is the websiteâ€™s equivalent of a physical address. Just as you would type in a GPS an address and it would direct you to the location, the same works for website domain names.

Directly responsible for the maintenance of the main repo of IP addresses and assists in the coordination of the reservoir of IP addresses. In addition to this meaningful task, ICANN also manages the domain name system and root servers and currently manages over 180 million domain names and four billion network addresses across 240 countries.

The reason why we use ICANN is because it is a non-profit organization dedicated to keeping the Internet secure, stable and compatible. It promotes competition and develops policy on the Internet's unique identifiers.
